Physics - Chapter 4: Machines (Page 1)

Questions and Answers

  1. What are the three functions of a simple machine?
    • A machine multiplies force.
    • A machine changes the direction of force.
    • A machine changes the speed of movement.
  2. List four types of simple machines.
    • Screw
    • Inclined Plane
    • Wedge
    • Pulley
  3. What is the principle of a lever? Write the formula for its mechanical advantage.

    The principle of a lever states that the turning effect of the effort at the fulcrum equals the turning effect of the load at the fulcrum.

    Formula: Effort × Effort Arm = Load × Load Arm

  4. Describe the three types of levers.
    • First-Class Lever: The fulcrum lies between the load and the effort. Example: Scissors, See-Saw
    • Second-Class Lever: The load lies between the fulcrum and the effort. Example: Nutcracker, Wheelbarrow
    • Third-Class Lever: The effort lies between the fulcrum and the load. Example: Tweezers, Tongs
  5. Name any two simple machines in each case where:
    • Multiply force: Screw Jack, Inclined Plane
    • Change the direction of force: Pulley, Lemon Squeezer
    • Apply force at a convenient point: Scissors, Screwdriver
  6. Give two applications of an inclined plane.
    • A gangplank is used to unload cargo from a ship.
    • A playground slide is an example of an inclined plane.
