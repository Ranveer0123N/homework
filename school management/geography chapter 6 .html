Geography Chapter 6: Study of Continents - North America

A. Short Answer Questions

  1. Name the water masses surrounding North America:
    • The Pacific Ocean (west)
    • The Atlantic Ocean (east)
    • The Arctic Ocean (north)
    • The Caribbean Sea (south)
  2. Which water body joins the Atlantic Ocean to the Pacific Ocean?
    • The Panama Canal.
  3. Name the three largest countries of North America:
    • Canada
    • U.S.A
    • Mexico
  4. What are the four main physical features of North America?
    • The Western Mountain Ranges
    • The Appalachian Mountains
    • The Great Plains
    • The Canadian Shield
  5. What is the most important river of North America?
    • The Mississippi River. Its tributaries include the Missouri, Ohio, and Arkansas Rivers.
  6. Where is softwood mainly found in North America?
    • Softwood is mainly found in Canada and is used in construction and paper pulp industries.
  7. What work do loggers do?
    • Loggers climb trees, cut branches, and use cables to bring down trees for timber.
  8. What is an intermontane plateau? Give a few examples.
    • A plateau enclosed by mountain ranges. Examples include the Columbia Plateau, the Great Basin, the Colorado Plateau, and the Mexican Plateau.
  9. Describe the Canadian Shield.
    • The Canadian Shield is in the northern part of the continent around Hudson Bay. It contains some of the oldest rocks in the world and is rich in metallic minerals.
  10. What are the prairies, and why are they important?
    • The prairies are temperate grasslands and are essential for agriculture. In Canada, they are called the Prairies, and they are drained by the Saskatchewan River and its tributaries.
  11. How does Canada protect its forests from logging?
    • Canada has strict laws and policies to protect its forests from destruction and ensure sustainability.
  12. Why are there many rapids and waterfalls along the eastern edge of the Piedmont?
    • The steep eastern edge of the Piedmont creates rapids and waterfalls as rivers flow from the Appalachian Mountains into the Atlantic Ocean.
